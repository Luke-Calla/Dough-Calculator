Baker's Percentages

At the heart of every great loaf is a mathematical constant. Baker's percentages define every ingredient as a ratio of total flour weight — the one fixed reference point in any formula.

This system eliminates the ambiguity of volume measurements and lets you scale a recipe without touching its underlying structure. Doubling a batch means doubling the flour; every other ingredient follows automatically.

Time and Temperature

The invisible hand of the baker is temperature. Yeast activity is not linear — it follows a curve shaped by the thermal energy within the dough mass and its environment.

Below 4°C fermentation stalls almost entirely, which is why a cold fridge rise can extend a schedule from hours to days. Above ~40°C yeast begins to die. Peak activity sits around 26–28°C for Saccharomyces cerevisiae.

The calculator uses lookup tables calibrated at multiple temperature and time intervals to interpolate the precise yeast activity for your conditions.
